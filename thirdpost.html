Gender Inequality in Nursing

Despite being a female-dominated profession, nursing still faces one of the most significant gender pay gaps

Posted by Susan Jacob on April 28, 2023

Nurses and nursing leaders have a crucial role to play in creating a culture of gender equity and advocating for systemic changes to address workplace gender discrimination in nursing.

Addressing workplace gender discrimination in nursing requires a multi-level approach, including policy changes, organizational culture change, and individual education and training.

Discriminatory behaviors include unequal pay, lack of career advancement opportunities, exclusion from decision-making processes, and harassment and bullying.

Discriminatory attitudes include gender stereotypes, bias, and prejudice, which can lead to women being undervalued, marginalized, and stereotyped in the workplace.

The consequences of workplace gender discrimination in nursing can be significant, including job dissatisfaction, burnout, decreased productivity, and compromised patient care.

Kaplan data

Addressing the pay gap in nursing is essential to promote gender equality and ensure that nurses are compensated fairly for their critical work. It is not only a matter of fairness but also crucial for the overall health of our society.
